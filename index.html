Singular and plural nouns

Para nombres singulares usamos (a / an) mientras que para el plural no.

Regular nouns

Most singular nouns from the plural by adding "s"
boat / boats
house / houses
cat / cats
river / rivers

A singular noun ending in (s, x, z, ch, sh, o) makes the plural by adding "es"
bus / buses
wish / wishes
pitch / pitches
box / boxes
potato / potatoes

If the name ends in a consonant + "y" we change the "y " to "i" and increase "es"
penny / pennies
spy / spies
baby / babies
city / cities
daisy / daisies

day / days

Spelling rules

Reglas gramaticales para la conjugación de verbos en presente


Terminan en (o, ch, sh, s, x, z) añadir "es"

En tercera persona singular, conjugar el verbo
go = goes | catch = catches | wash = washes
kiss = kisses | fix = fixes | do = does
I fix computers = reparo / arreglo computadoras
Marcos fixes computers = Marcos repera computadoras


Terminan en (consonante + y) reemplazar "y", por "ies"

Cambiamos la "y" por la "i" y añadimos "es"
marry = marries | study = studies
carry = carries | worry = worries
Martha and Peter study systems engineering = Ellos estudian ingenieria en sistemas
Martha studies systems engineering = Martha estudia ingeniaria en sistemas


Note: para verbos que terminan en vocal + "y", solo añadir "s"

I play | she plays
we enjoy | he enjoys
they say | it says


El resto de los verbos solo añadir "s"

I work | he works
we love | my dog loves
they like | Martha likes
you download | Carlos downloads
I surf | she surfs
